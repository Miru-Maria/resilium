UX & Usability
Immediate friction points:

The "UX Testing" link appears in the account dropdown for all signed-in users, not just admins — a potential security and confusion issue
The landing page stat "1M+ Scenarios Simulated" has no grounding. On a credibility-first product, that kind of fabricated metric damages trust more than it helps
The About page says "no account required" but saving a plan requires one — that copy should be reconciled
Anonymous (unauthenticated) reports are generated and stored indefinitely — there's no cleanup mechanism, which will bloat the database over time
After finishing an assessment without being signed in, there's no strong prompt to sign in and save the plan before the user navigates away — you're losing conversions right at the most motivated moment
The profile page only shows score + date. No location, no "Financial: 72 / Health: 45"-style preview. Users can't quickly remember what each plan was about without opening it
No rate limiting on report generation — a script could generate thousands of AI reports in minutes
Good things worth keeping:

The assessment flow is clean, fast, and visually polished. The 1-5 Likert scale with the large tap targets works well
The radar chart comparison is genuinely differentiating
The checklist / snapshot system on the results page is high-retention
Design
The landing page is a single screen of hero content, then 3 stats, then footer. There's no "How it works", no social proof, no example output — you're asking people to start a 10-question assessment with zero persuasion below the fold
The mobile app and web app have different step counts (10 vs 11) and the mobile app lacks auth/plan saving — these two products are diverging in capability
PDF export quality should be audited — if it's a raw browser print it's likely ugly
Monetization
The current model is fully free with no revenue path. Here's a realistic stack, ordered by effort-to-revenue:

Stripe "Support Resilium" donation button on the results page — lowest effort, some conversion from satisfied users
Freemium tier — "Resilium Pro" ($4.99/month or $39/year):
Unlimited saved plans (free tier stays at 3, not 10)
Styled PDF export with cover page and branding
Re-assessment reminders by email ("Your plan is 90 days old")
Progress line chart over all your plans
Priority AI processing
Team/Org plans ($29/month for up to 25 members): HR teams doing business continuity planning, NGOs, community organisations
Affiliate recommendations on the results page: Emergency kit suppliers, budgeting tools (YNAB, Monarch), insurance comparison sites — contextual to the user's risk profile (e.g., low financial score → link to budgeting tools)
B2B data insights: Sell anonymised, aggregated quarterly reports on "UK resilience trends" to insurers, local councils, think tanks
Marketing & Audience
Core audiences:

Financial anxiety — r/personalfinance, r/UKPersonalFinance, FIRE community. Hook: "How many months could you survive a job loss?"
Preppers and preparedness — r/preppers, r/survivalism, Prepared.com audience. Currently underserved by polished, non-paranoid tools
Expats and digital nomads — highly mobile, uncertain about location risk, financial contingency planning
Climate-affected regions — people in flood zones, wildfire areas, politically unstable regions
HR and corporate — business continuity, employee wellbeing, pandemic planning post-COVID
Channels:

SEO content: "How prepared are you for a recession?", "Personal resilience score", "Emergency preparedness quiz" — these are low-competition, high-intent search terms
Shareable score card: When users get their result, let them generate a shareable image ("I scored 68/100 on the Resilium Readiness Index. How do you score?") — organic social distribution built into the product itself
Product Hunt launch: The AI + privacy-first angle is compelling for that audience
Newsletter partnerships: Financial independence, emergency prep, and expat newsletters would feature this tool
Admin
No user management panel — you can't see who your users are, how many plans they've run, or block a bad actor
No email notification when a GDPR request comes in — you could miss a legally time-sensitive request
No CSV export of analytics data
The UX tester admin link is exposed in the public dropdown
No way to write a site-wide announcement (e.g., "We've updated our methodology — re-assess to see your updated score")
