================================================================================
  RESILIUM — DEMO VIDEO PRODUCTION GUIDE
  Full Script, Narration Guide & Production Cues
================================================================================

  Format:     Screen recording + voiceover (desktop, optionally mobile)
  Duration:   ~3 min 35 sec
  Tone:       Calm, authoritative, modern SaaS
  Music:      Minimal lo-fi ambient — keep below 20% volume under narration
  Resolution: 1920×1080 preferred / 1280×720 minimum

================================================================================
  BEFORE YOU RECORD — TECHNICAL SETUP
================================================================================

  BROWSER / APP
  - Log in to the app fresh before recording. Confirm you have at least two
    saved reports in your profile (needed for Scene 4).
  - Use a clean browser profile (no extensions visible, no notification
    banners).
  - Set zoom to 100%. Hide the browser bookmark bar.
  - Pre-fill nothing — you'll type and click live on camera.

  SCREEN
  - Hide the taskbar / dock. Use full-screen or a maximised window.
  - Close all unnecessary tabs. Pin only the Resilium tab.
  - Turn off notifications (macOS: Focus / Do Not Disturb, Windows: Quiet Hours).

  CURSOR
  - Use a larger-than-default cursor (OS accessibility settings).
  - All cursor moves are SLOW and deliberate — hover briefly before clicking.
  - NEVER use keyboard shortcuts on screen (no Cmd+T, etc.).

  ANSWERS TO USE DURING RECORDING
  - Location:         London, UK
  - Income:           Freelance / Variable
  - Financial buffer: ~3 months (slide roughly to the middle)
  - Dependents:       Yes
  - Skills:           Digital / Tech   +   First Aid / Medical
  - Housing:          Renting long-term
  - Emergency supplies: No
  - Risk concerns:    Job Loss   +   Hyperinflation
  Note: These choices are calibrated to produce a score in the 45–60 range
  ("Moderately Prepared"), ensuring all vulnerability and action sections
  appear fully populated on camera.

  MENTAL RESILIENCE ANSWERS
  Rate the ten statements so that your total sits roughly in the mid-range
  (score ~60–70/100 on that dimension). Mix 3s, 4s and a couple of 2s.
  Avoid all 5s — the mid-range produces more interesting recommendations.

================================================================================
  VOICEOVER DELIVERY GUIDE
================================================================================

  GENERAL STYLE
  - Pace: ~140 words per minute. Not a rush, not a drawl.
  - Pitch: Mid-to-low. Avoid rising inflection at the end of sentences.
  - Emphasis: Bold words carry natural emphasis — do not over-stress them.
  - Breath: Take a full breath before every new scene. It resets your tone
    and is inaudible after editing.
  - Silence: Respect the [PAUSE] cues. Silence is not dead air — it lets
    the visual breathe and the viewer process.

  SCENE TONES AT A GLANCE
  - Scene 1: Weighty opener → pivots to relief/solution
  - Scene 2: Informative guide — your voice is the friendly expert
  - Scene 3: Clinical then energising — diagnosis → prescription
  - Scene 4: Warm, personal — this is the user's data, their history
  - Scene 5: Operator perspective — slightly more formal/technical
  - Scene 6: Tagline cadence — spare, deliberate, final

  WORDS TO EMPHASISE (bold = slight emphasis, CAPS = clear emphasis)
  - Scene 1: "CONSTANT disruption", "Resilium changes THAT"
  - Scene 2: "eleven dimensions", "UNLOCK growth-oriented"
  - Scene 3: "six WEIGHTED categories", "NOT a generic template",
             "CRITICAL down to low risk", "Fully Resilient"
  - Scene 4: "over TIME", "visible AT A GLANCE"
  - Scene 5: "WITHOUT waiting for real user feedback"
  - Scene 6: "Resilium." [pause] Each sentence as its own beat.

  COMMON MISTAKES
  - Don't speed up during the skill and risk concern lists in Scene 2 —
    they need to feel thorough, not rattled off.
  - Don't let your voice drop on the final word of a sentence unless it's
    deliberate (like the closing tagline).
  - Don't re-record individual words and stitch them — re-record full
    sentences or full scenes for natural prosody.

================================================================================
  POST-PRODUCTION NOTES
================================================================================

  EDITING
  - Cut between scenes on motion — cut while the cursor is moving toward
    the next page, not after it arrives. Keeps energy up.
  - The loading spinner in Scene 2 can be trimmed to 2–3 seconds in edit
    even if it took longer during recording.
  - Colour grade: slight warmth (+5 temperature) and lift in the blacks to
    match the app's dark-warm aesthetic (#0D1225 background, #E08040
    primary orange).

  CAPTIONS
  - Add auto-generated captions and review them for accuracy on all
    proper nouns: Resilium, hyperinflation, geopolitical.
  - Caption style: white text, dark semi-transparent background strip,
    centered, 80% viewport width maximum.

  MUSIC
  - Recommended BPM: 75–90 (ambient lo-fi, no prominent melody)
  - Volume: -25 dB under narration, -18 dB during silent pauses
  - Fade out music entirely under the closing tagline (Scene 6)
  - Fade back in under the logo card at the very end

  THUMBNAIL
  - Frame: Results page showing the radar chart fully visible
  - Score: "54 / 100 — Moderately Prepared" visible in the gauge
  - Text overlay: "Are you resilient enough?" in app brand font
  - Background: app's dark theme, no additional graphics needed
